# Course Discord Access

The SaaS-Pro Discord is where you get unstuck when a lesson doesn't behave
the way the video says it should. It's also where you'll see other
students shipping their Contactly builds and where course updates are
announced.

## How to join

`[invite link issued in your welcome email]`

When you purchased the course, an invite link was emailed to you from the
same address you used to check out. If you can't find it, check spam, then
contact support from the same email address so we can verify the
purchase.

The invite is single-use per account. Don't share it — the Discord is
gated to paying students and we audit membership monthly.

## Channels you'll see

Once you're in, the server is organised around where you are in the
course and what kind of help you need.

### General

- **#announcements** — course updates, new lessons, and any breaking
  changes to the stack. Read-only for students; instructor posts only.
- **#help** — general questions that don't fit a specific module. If
  you're not sure where to post, post here.
- **#show-your-work** — post your Contactly build. Deployed URL, a
  screenshot, whatever you've got. This is the single most motivating
  channel on the server.
- **#off-topic** — chat, links, memes. Keep it friendly.

### Module channels

One channel per module, for questions specific to that module's
lessons:

- **#module-01** through **#module-13**

When you post in a module channel, you know everyone reading has the same
context you do. That makes answers faster and more precise.

## Community guidelines

Three rules. They're short because they cover everything.

1. **Be kind.** Assume the person asking a question is smart, tired, and
   on their third hour of debugging. Answer accordingly.
2. **No piracy.** Don't share course videos, course content, invite
   links, or your account. If a friend wants in, send them to the
   landing page.
3. **Post lesson references when asking for help.** Every lesson has a
   module and lesson number (e.g. "3.2" for module 3, lesson 2). Include
   it in your question. "I'm stuck" is impossible to help with. "I'm on
   3.2, the Supabase sign-in is redirecting to `/` instead of
   `/app/contacts`, here's my code" gets an answer in minutes.

## Response-time expectations

Be realistic: this is a course Discord, not a paid support desk.

- **Instructor checks weekdays.** Monday through Friday, usually in the
  morning in my timezone. Weekends are best-effort.
- **Other students answer faster than I do** — and often better, because
  they just solved the thing yesterday. Don't wait for me; engage with
  replies from peers.
- **Urgent production issues on your own deployed Contactly** are not
  something the course Discord can solve. The course teaches you to
  operate your own SaaS; once you're in production, you're on the hook
  for your own uptime.

## Your first post

Once you're in, head to **#show-your-work** and say hi. A one-line
introduction, what you're hoping to ship after the course, and where
you're based. That's enough. The server is more useful once you've made
yourself slightly vulnerable to strangers on the internet. Welcome
aboard.
